    <h2>AI, pricing, and digital competition</h2>
    <p>
      My job market paper develops a mechanism of <strong>dominant
      followership</strong> arising from demand and timing asymmetries. When a
      smaller firm moves first, it can raise its price anticipating that a
      demand-advantaged follower will match rather than undercut the increase.
      This alignment of firms' preferred roles can sustain higher prices,
      benefiting both firms at the expense of consumer welfare.
    </p>
